The Rural Reality & My Vision

Teaching in rural Telangana presents unique challenges. When I began, we faced extreme geographic isolation, a severe lack of formal laboratory infrastructure, and deeply rooted social misconceptions. My vision was simple but ambitious: I wanted to transform our everyday village environment into a living scientific laboratory.

Aligned with the National Curriculum Framework for School Education (NCFSE) 2023, I shifted our classroom focus entirely away from rote learning and heavily toward logical reasoning and evidence-based thinking.

Identification & Mentorship

Our journey begins early. I identify my students not by their test grades, but by their raw curiosity.

My Strategy: I track and mentor them longitudinally from Grade 6 all the way to 10. This allows for the gradual, deep development of their "Scientific Temper." It is never about rushing a single project; it is a 5-year cultivation of a mindset.

The Innovation Era

2005 ‚Äì 2015

Technologies & Resources:

I introduced my "Experiments Teacher" model. We started using low-cost, locally available village materials to bypass the need for expensive lab equipment.

My Pedagogy:

I led a shift toward hands-on learning and practical application. Abstract concepts were suddenly proven using everyday items my students recognized.

Horizons for Children

Breaking the district barrier: My students realized their ideas had value. They began competing and winning at State Levels (e.g., INSPIRE Awards), gaining confidence to look beyond our mandal.
